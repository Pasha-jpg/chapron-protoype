Henri Chapron is a Carroissieri, or coach builder who has produced some of the most beautiful and exquisite cars. Originally located in Neuilly, France in 1919, Henri Chapron moved to Levallois-Perret, Siene in 1927 and worked there until his retirement in 1986. After the introduction of the Citroën DS in 1955, Citroën wanted to introduce a small number of DS19 Cabriolets.
Henri Chapron understood, based on its appearance, that the DS could offer him a variety of interesting models. To entice a significant part of the Citroën clientèle, he proposed several designs, on the basis of the saloon version of the DS 19, to develop luxury cars jointly with Citroën.

History
Chapron hand built a small number of cars based on Citroën’s mass produced cars, creating a prestigious range of cars for the elite. In October 1958, on Chapron’s stand at the motor show, a strange DS makes its appearance: A cabriolet christened the Croisette, which seated four.
As a result of the motor show, Henri Chapron create a second version of the DS, the LeParis whose clean lines would spark the creation of many more models from the workshops of the rue Aristide-Briand .
In 1959, Henri Chapron built a prototype cabriolet 2 + 2 based on the Citroën DS, a lighter version of the Croisette. As a result of this proposed by the coach builder, Pierre Bercot, director general of Citroën, decided to integrate the cabriolet DS into the catalogue range from 1960. Chapron would provide the actual manufacture of the cabriolets.
So started a great working relationship with Citroën and Henri Chapron. Chapron went onto create special DS saloon models called “Prestige” which included full leather, a bar, a chauffeur window and more. Other models include the LeDandy, Palm Beach, Majesty, LeMan, Lorraine, Opera, Mylord and the special Landaulet models for the SM and CX. Chapron created the Presidentialle models as well.
